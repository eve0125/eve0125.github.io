The Coerced Confessions


Every year, hundreds of people are falsely convicted due to outdated and coercive police tactics.
That's something that has to change.

  • Learn More
  • Support Us

In most courts today, confessions get convictions. If someone confesses, they likely did the crime, and they should be punished for it. While this should be the case, in the US, current police interrogation tactics make it very easy to gain false confessions, especially when these tactics are used against children and the mentally disabled.

Here, we want to inform you of the enormous flaws in our justice system, equip you with the tools to fight for change, and engage you in petitions and movements in your community to bring the fight close to home.
